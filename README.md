🧠 Mental Health Analytics Dashboard
📌 Overview

The Mental Health Analytics Dashboard is an interactive Excel-based analytics project designed to explore mental health survey data and uncover insights related to stress levels, mental health conditions, workload, sleep, exercise habits, and treatment-seeking behavior.

The project transforms raw survey responses into meaningful business intelligence that can support organizations, healthcare professionals, and policymakers in understanding key drivers of mental well-being.

🎯 Objective

To analyze mental health data and identify patterns between:

Workload and stress levels
Sleep and exercise habits
Mental health conditions and treatment-seeking behavior
Country-level differences in stress
🛠️ Tools & Technologies
Microsoft Excel
Pivot Tables
Pivot Charts
Data Cleaning & Transformation
Dashboard Design
📊 Dataset Overview

The dataset contains responses from 638 participants with the following attributes:

Variable	Description
Age	Participant age
Gender	Gender identity
Country	Country of residence
Mental Health Condition	Reported condition
Stress Level	Self-reported stress score
Sleep Hours	Daily sleep duration
Weekly Work Hours	Workload indicator
Exercise Frequency	Physical activity level
Job Satisfaction	Satisfaction rating
Treatment Status	Whether treatment is sought

Structuring data for analysis and visualization

📊 Key Findings
1. Overall Stress Levels

The average stress level was 5.63 / 10, indicating moderate stress across participants.

2. Most Common Mental Health Conditions
Condition	Count
PTSD	91
Schizophrenia	91
Depression	76
Anxiety	71
Bipolar Disorder	68

👉 PTSD and Schizophrenia were the most frequently reported conditions.

3. Treatment-Seeking Behavior
Category	Percentage
Seeking Treatment	25.4%
Not Seeking Treatment	24.0%
No Response	50.6%

👉 A large portion of participants did not disclose treatment status.

4. Workload vs Mental Health
Depression: 51.57 average weekly hours
Anxiety: 47.21 hours
Higher workloads were associated with increased stress levels.
5. Sleep & Exercise Impact
Lower sleep duration correlated with higher stress levels
Regular exercise was linked to improved mental well-being
Poor lifestyle habits increased stress indicators
6. Country-Level Stress Comparison
Country	Avg Stress Level
Germany	6.11
Canada	5.89
Australia	5.22
United States	5.22
France	5.07
💡 Insights
Mental health conditions are strongly influenced by workload and lifestyle habits
Sleep and exercise are key protective factors
Significant variation exists across countries
Treatment-seeking behavior is relatively low
